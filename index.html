History Of The Marathon

Contrary to the popular belief that the marathon takes part in the Classical World, the marathon is actually a modern invention. While the Ancient Olympics can be dated back to 776 BC, there is no evidence of a 26 mile foot race ever taking place. The marathon was, however, included in the first modern Olympics in Athens in 1896. At the first olympic games, eighteen men lined up at the starting line. In the 2016 marathon, 62 men lined up and finished under 2:20:00. The first New York marathon in 1970 saw 127 entrants and in 1979 there were 11,500. In 2015 there were 50,000 entrants in the New York marathon.
